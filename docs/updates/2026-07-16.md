# Patch Notes — 15–16 July 2026

## Castle Positions and KvK Applications

**Who is affected:** KvK applicants, Kings, Ministers of Justice, and Supreme Admins.

- Castle Positions now supports the public application-to-schedule workflow: player linking, UTC availability, speedup and True Gold declarations, stage eligibility/ranking, a time-by-position planner, manual placement, and reviewed placement suggestions.
- Administrators can configure stage positions and capacity, save a draft, finalize it for review, publish a numbered player-visible schedule, reopen it for changes, and publish a revised version. Draft and finalized schedules are not player-visible.
- Publication can notify opted-in applicants through Kingdom Castle Positions email settings. Delivery may be skipped or fail when contact details or sender configuration are unavailable; players should also check their overview.

Read the [Castle Positions guides](/castle-positions/overview), [application statuses](/castle-positions/application-statuses), and [schedule planning](/castle-positions/schedule-planner).

## Roles and access

- Ministers of Justice can operate Castle Positions only for their assigned Kingdom. This role does not provide general platform administration.
- Castle Positions administration is available to the in-scope King, Minister of Justice, or Supreme Admin. Players can see their own application and appointments but cannot edit the schedule.

## Registration and email

- Public applicants can link a verified existing account or submit with optional guest contact where the Kingdom email sender is available.
- Notification contact is distinct from a sign-in account. Email availability is checked for guest contact, and schedule email is optional rather than a requirement to apply.
- Account-recovery and welcome-email handling was strengthened alongside this work. Users should continue to use the password-reset and registration flows rather than sharing credentials with administrators.

## Scheduling and planning

- The planner now supports a real time-by-position grid, drag-and-drop assignments, manual player search, clearer slot actions, avatar placeholders, and density controls for large schedules.
- Suggestions remain a review tool. They consider availability, eligibility, ranking, capacity, and existing assignments, but an administrator must review and save the result.

## Subscription and usage improvements

- Subscription usage responses and the administration view were made more consistent. Usage details can load on demand and may show a loading or error state while data is retrieved.

## Reliability fixes and user management

- The application tightened inactive-user visibility and improved recovery matching and account-email handling. Deleted or disabled accounts may no longer appear in normal lists; restore or account-resolution work belongs to an administrator.
- Castle schedule validation, slot coverage, lifecycle revisions, and display/accessibility details were improved. Players should treat the published version—not a draft or provisional board—as the reliable schedule.

## Known limitations

- A preferred time is a request, not a guaranteed slot. Capacity and review can leave an accepted candidate unplaced.
- Email delivery depends on enabled Kingdom settings, valid opted-in contact, and successful delivery.
- A stage without a date or configured positions cannot build a usable schedule.
- Automatic placement suggestions do not publish or confirm appointments.
