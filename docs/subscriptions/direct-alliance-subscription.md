# Direct Alliance Subscriptions

An alliance can get premium two ways: through a [grant](kingdom-grants.md) from its King, or by having its **own** subscription — a **direct alliance subscription**. This page explains the "own plan" route and why an alliance might prefer it.

## What "direct" means

A direct alliance subscription is a [plan](../getting-started/glossary.md#plan) that belongs to the **alliance itself**, not borrowed from the kingdom. On your usage panel, its **plan source** reads **"Direct alliance plan"** — the strongest source in the [effective-plan order](effective-plan.md).

You get one the same way as any premium plan: [request it](request-subscription.md) and arrange [payment](payment-instructions.md) with a Supreme Admin.

## Direct plan vs. kingdom grant

Both give an alliance premium, but they behave differently:

| | Direct alliance plan | Accepted kingdom grant |
|---|---|---|
| Belongs to | The alliance | The kingdom (shared with you) |
| How you get it | Request + arrange payment yourself | Your King offers it; you accept |
| Features | The full alliance premium set | Only the **shareable** features the grant carries |
| If the King changes plans | Unaffected — it's yours | Can end if the King revokes the grant |
| Survives a kingdom **usage-limit** pause | **Yes** | No (unless separately exempted) |

## The big advantage: surviving a kingdom usage-limit suspension

If the **kingdom** hits a hard limit and drops into a usage-limit [suspension](suspension.md), alliances under it are normally paused too — **except** an alliance with its **own direct paid plan, which keeps working**. Its own subscription shields it from the kingdom's usage-limit pause.

This is the main reason an alliance that wants reliable, independent premium — not tied to the kingdom's ups and downs — chooses a direct subscription.

> This shield applies specifically to **usage-limit** kingdom suspensions. The harder administrative reasons (manual, admin, abuse, global) can still cascade unless a Supreme Admin grants a specific override. See the cascade rules in [Suspension & Limited Mode](suspension.md).

## Which should an alliance choose?

- **Choose a grant** if your King is happy to share the kingdom's premium and you're fine depending on it. It's the no-cost-to-you route — see [Accept a Premium Offer](accept-grant.md).
- **Choose a direct plan** if you want the full alliance feature set, independence from the kingdom's plan, and protection from a kingdom usage-limit pause.

There's no wrong answer — it depends on how independent your alliance wants to be.
